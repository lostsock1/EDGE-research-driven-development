# Superior {{PROJECT_NAME}} Architecture — Evidence-Weighted North Star

> **What this is.** The theoretical best architecture for {{PROJECT_NAME}}, maintained as a
> living research artifact **outside** the implementation repo. It is the north star the
> shipping roadmap is pressure-tested against — not a backlog, not a status doc, not a
> defense of the current design.
>
> **Scope discipline — read this first.** This document assumes **realistic-but-generous
> hardware and full access to all available models**. It is **not** bound to the current
> deployment target. Latency numbers, RAM ceilings, cost, OOM, and current-hardware limits
> are *deployment* concerns tracked in the repo (`{{DOCS_DIR}}/`) and `RESUME.md` — cite them
> here **only** where they expose a hardware-independent *mechanism* truth, and **never** as
> an architecture constraint. If a claim would change on a bigger box, it does not belong here.
>
> **Two inputs, one synthesis.** This doc is fed by BOTH **external research** (papers,
> repos, production practice, benchmarks) AND **internal evidence** (this project's own
> evals, ADRs, contained lab experiments, and coder reality-feedback). Internal findings
> are the higher grade of evidence because the researcher could not shape the test — but
> each is distilled to the mechanism truth that survives on any hardware before it lands here.
>
> **Evidence weights.** Tag every architectural claim: **proven** (field-measured) ·
> **field-consensus** (multiple production systems) · **promising** (single paper/benchmark) ·
> **speculative** (theory only). An unweighted claim is not done.
>
> **Persona:** {{PERSONA}} — conjecture boldly, criticize without mercy, make every detail
> load-bearing or delete it; make the explanation hard to vary.

## How this doc is created and maintained

- **Created** at project kickoff — seeded from the template into
  `projects/{{PROJECT_SLUG}}/notes/SUPERIOR_ARCHITECTURE.md` — then rewritten as evidence
  arrives. It is authored by the research agent, not generated by any script.
- **Revise it when** either input changes: (a) external research surfaces a usable
  mechanism or a stronger rival, OR (b) an internal eval / ADR / lab experiment / coder
  reality-feedback changes what "best" means. Do a synthesis pass; do not let it drift
  behind the dated notes that feed it.
- **Relationship to the shipping roadmap.** The repo's `ROADMAP.md`/`TASKS.md` is the
  *constrained, ship-it* track. This doc is the *unconstrained best-known* track. They run
  in parallel and must not blur.
- **Promotion is one-directional and gated.** A finding crosses from here into the repo
  **only** as a slot-replacement packet through `KNOWLEDGE_STAGING.md` →
  `RESEARCH_TRANSFER.md` → `TASKS.md`/ADR (name the slot, baseline, candidate, stable
  contract, gates, migration/rollback, stop trigger). Raw north-star prose is never a
  coding instruction.
- **Feedback flows back.** When a coder tests a promoted mechanism against the real code
  (`EDGE_COLLABORATION.md`), fold the outcome back here: a rejection is a result, and an
  implemented-and-proven mechanism is evidence the design is real (say so, and move the
  open frontier forward).
- **The null is valid.** "No change to the north star this cycle" is a legitimate result.
  Do not manufacture revisions on a schedule.
